Argentina

Argentina is an exciting country full of contrasts. The vibrant city of Buenos Aires, imposing glaciers, breathtaking waterfalls, colorful mountains, salt flats, lakes, forests, diverse wildlife, and the "departure gate" to Antarctica from the southernmost city in the planet. Get ready to taste unique gastronomy with superb meats and wines, go to a tango show and even try your first steps of this sensual dance! Enjoy countless horseback riding adventures or sit back and watch the best polo tournaments in the world.

Buenos Aires

A city of passion

Explore this vibrant and fascinating city you will fall in love with!

Buenos Aires is, undoubtedly, one of the most interesting capitals in Latin America. Dynamic and cosmopolitan, it stands out for its rich cultural and architectural heritage.

Puerto Madryn

Wildlife at its best

Puerto Madryn is a small coastal city in Patagonia and a great destination for wildlife lovers. It is the gate to Peninsula Valdés (World Heritage Site by UNESCO), a unique Nature Reserve of global significance for the conservation of marine mammals. You can also visit Punta Tombo, one of the largest Magellanic penguin colonies in the world.

Ushuaia

End of the World

Discover the legendary and untamed Land of Fire

Lying on the shores of the Beagle Channel, on the Main Island of Tierra del Fuego, Ushuaia is considered the southernmost city in the world. It is a place surrounded by fantastic landscapes and the closest port to Antarctica.

Ushuaia at dawn

Les Eclaireurs lighthouse

Explore its singular National Park where you can enjoy a combination of Sub Antarctic forest, mountains of the Andes Range and marine environment.

End of the World Train

Take a boat trip across the mythical Beagle Channel and admire the unique views of the city and the magnificent scenery around it.

King crab

Treat yourself to local cuisine. Ushuaia is an excellent place to enjoy fresh seafood, particularly king crab!

El Chaltén

Mountain paradise

Feel the energy of this magical destination!

El Chaltén is a small village located at the foot of the spectacular Mount Fitz Roy, in Los Glaciares National Park. Known as Argentina’s trekking capital, there are multiple outdoor activities to enjoy the fresh, unpolluted air and fantastic scenery.

Bariloche & The Lake District

Adventure and a picture-perfect scenery all year round!

Bariloche is a city located in Nahuel Huapi National Park, next to the Andes in northwest Patagonia. Surrounded by millenary forests, mountains covered in snow and crystal-clear lakes, it is the ideal starting point to explore the region and enjoy outdoor activities.

Mendoza

The cradle of Malbec

Discover Mendoza’s Wine Route and its spectacular scenery!

Located at the foothill of the Andes, Mendoza is a world-renowned Wine Capital. The great diversity of soils, climate and altitude grants the production of different high-quality wines with specific characteristics depending on the growing area.

It is a wonderful destination for foodies due to its wide variety of wineries and gourmet restaurants where European traditions blend with local flavors.

Puerto Iguazú

Extraordinary nature

Explore the lush subtropical rainforest and feel the force of nature in this stunning natural scenery.

Puerto Iguazú is located on the northeast of Argentina, bordering Brazil and Paraguay where the rivers Paraná & Iguazú meet. From here you can visit the majestic Iguazu Falls, declared World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

Iberá Wetlands

A unique ecosystem

Immerse yourself in nature like in no other place!

The Iberá Park, “Shiny Water” in Guaraní (local language), is a beautiful place located in the heart of Corrientes Province in the Northeast of Argentina. Here Nature expresses herself in a unique way. Formed by a complex network of wetlands, marshes, swamplands, lagoons, reservoirs and pluvial streams it has more than 700,000 protected hectares. It is one of the largest parks in Argentina and the second largest wetland in the world! High biodiversity and a great variety of landscapes blend in perfectly with the gaucho way of life.
