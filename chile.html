Chile

Fire, ice, mystery and a wine paradise. Chile offers all of this and much more! Visit the magnificent Atacama Desert, explore astonishing lakes, forests, glaciers and volcanoes in Patagonia and head to Antarctica from Punta Arenas. Discover the mysterious Easter Island and do not miss the outstanding wines produced in the Central Region of the country. These are just some of the numerous Chilean treasures. The longest country in the world awaits you!

Santiago & Central Coast

Hustle and bustle & ocean breezes

Discover the capital of Chile and enjoy the breeze of the Pacific Ocean

Located at the foot of the Andes Mountains, Santiago is a captivating city full of contrasts between tradition and modernity. Stroll along the traditional city center, visit the modern eastern districts and the Metropolitan Park that offers unique views of the Andes. Discover the city’s cultural activity, first-class gastronomy and lively nightlife. Last but certainly not least, during wintertime Santiago is the base for skiing in the most beautiful and largest ski resorts of the Southern Hemisphere.

Santiago de Chile - copyright Sernatur

Valparaíso - copyright Sernatur

Valparaíso - copyright Sernatur

Only 1.5 hours west of the capital you can feel the ocean breeze while wandering around the narrow streets of Valparaiso, a bohemian and vivid port. The city is characterized by its more than 40 hills as well as more than 15 funiculars, and its historic quarter is nominated World Heritage Site. Be invited to visit remarkable art galleries and delight yourself at a local restaurant with exquisite dishes prepared with fresh seafood.

Lauca National Park and Chungara Lake

Deep Andes and native culture

Travel across the Andean high plateau of the extreme northern Chile

Starting from Arica on the seaside, located at the extreme North of Chile, you will be able to visit the Archaeological Museum of San Miguel de Azapa that has the oldest mummies on the planet.

Wild Alpaca

Afterwards, you will reach the high plateaus where the Lauca National Park and Chungara lake are surrounded by ancient volcanoes and inhabited by the ancestral native culture, the Aymaras. Immerse yourself in the real Andes and soak up the atmosphere of the picturesque villages of Putre, Virviri and Parinacota. Meet a local shaman to hear their fascinating stories. Observe the magnificent local fauna of the region: vicuñas, Andean foxes and a wide variety of bird species.

San Pedro de Atacama

Atacama Desert, wild desertic sceneries, stargazing and native culture

Visit one of the most peculiar places on Earth. A Chilean must!

Located in the heart of the Atacama Desert, San Pedro de Atacama is an ancient village, nurtured by the energy of welcoming ancestral cultures. It is also an oasis, a vital source of life in the driest desert in the world.

Cejar Lagoon

Desert landscape

Stay in luxury lodges or in simpler yet fantastic accommodations and explore this incredible area with amazing local gastronomy.

Moon Valley – copyright Sernatur

Get to know unique landscapes in the world: salt flats, geysers, the Moon Valley, volcanoes, and deep blue Altiplanic lagoons as well as fascinating archaeological sites.

Tatio Geyser - copyright Sernatur

Get ready to spot the local fauna: pink flamingos, vicuñas (wild llamas), condors, Ñandues (altitude wild ostriches). Meet the native Kunzas, also called Licanrantay.

Embark on a journey through the stars. Atacama is a place where the skies are the clearest of the world and many international observatories are located there. Learn about ancestral star observation from the Atacameños, who developed this knowledge long before the arrival of the Spaniards.

San Pedro de Atacama is the gateway to the Bolivian Uyuni Salt Flat as well as to the Northwest of Argentina. These destinations are a perfect combination to explore this stunning area of South America.

Elqui Valley

The “Small” Desert. El Desierto Chico

Cradle of the Chilean Pisco

From the seaside city of La Serena towards the Elqui Valley, you can taste amazing seafood and enjoy fabulous stargazing spots. You can also visit the main distilleries producing Pisco, base of Chile's national drink, the Pisco Sour, which you will be able to try several times during your stay in the country.

Central Region Wine Valleys

Chilean fine wines at a stroll

Taste the charm and flavors of the Central Region wine valleys

Maipú, Pirque, Casablanca, Aconcagua, Cachapoal, Colchagua are some of the most renowned wine-producing areas around Santiago.

The excellent natural conditions endow the area with a superb wine production. The most celebrated vine is the Chilean Carmenere but the Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah, Pinot Noir and Merlot are also worth mentioning. And as white wines are concerned, Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Viognier stand out.

Casablanca Valley – copyright Sernatur

Visit different vineyards across the region tasting delicious wines and exquisite cuisine! You can also choose to stay in small boutique hotels and enjoy other activities such as horseback riding, cycling, or hiking amongst the vineyards.

Auraucanía

The place for outdoor lovers!

Enjoy the beauty of nature

Pucón is a small town located in the middle of the Chilean Araucanía. It is a land of active volcanoes, forests, rivers and lakes, and also the heartland of the native Mapuche people. Renowned as one of the best destinations for outdoor activities, you can enjoy hiking and trekking, mountain climbing, rafting, ziplining, as well as skiing and snowboarding during the winter months.

Marvel at millenary araucaria forests, explore amazing flora and fauna, relax at one the countless hot springs or visit local Mapuche sites to learn more about their culture and traditions.

Lake District: Puerto Varas and Chiloé Island

Nature, culture, and southern traditions

Explore the charms of this region

Located only 30 minutes away from Puerto Montt Airport, Puerto Varas is a small town on the shores of Llanquihue lake facing the imponent Osorno Volcano, in the center of the Chilean Lake District.

Puerto Varas - copyright Sernatur

Adventure, Chilean-German traditions and impressive nature is what Puerto Varas is about. Traverse the area's magical corners, its inviting waterfront overlooking the volcanoes, and enjoy the local culture and dining options.

Frutillar - copyright Sernatur

Chiloé - copyright Sernatur

You will be able to see waterfalls, volcanoes, lakes and other natural beauties along the way to the neighbouring island of Chiloé. This island, associated with many legends, has its famous stilt houses and wooden churches that have been declared World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

Chiloé Church - copyright Sernatur

From Puerto Varas, you may reach Bariloche in Argentina with the Lake Crossing tour, sailing across stunning waters and beautiful landscapes. Another alternative is to travel by land, visiting the low Andes and remarkable National Parks like Vicente Perez Rosales, the oldest one in Chile.

The Austral Highway

Scenic route

Discover a piece of paradise!

The Austral Highway (Carretera Austral) may be the best kept secret of Northern Patagonia. This scenic road connects with the Argentinian National Route 40 towards the south. This unique road, frequently used by adventurers, will lead you to Southern Patagonia through forests, fjords, glaciers and remote villages.

Stay in exclusive lodges and feel like an explorer coming across extraordinary landscapes such as the Marble Chapels, San Rafael Glacier and the faraway village of Caleta Tortel, built on stilts. This area also offers the best spots for flyfishing in Chile, a real paradise! Sail, drive, hike…just enjoy!

Torres del Paine National Park and Punta Arenas

Heaven for trekkers and gateway to Antarctica

Marvel at the natural wonders this corner of the world has to offer!

Located on the shores of the Strait of Magellan, Punta Arenas may just be a stopover before heading to Torres del Paine National Park, Tierra del Fuego or Antarctica. But it can also be a fantastic place for whale watching, for spotting King and Magellan Penguins, visiting history and anthropology museums and even staying overnight at a typical Patagonian Estancia.

Undoubtedly, Torres del Paine is an unmissable destination in Chile. Thanks to its unique landscape with glaciers, granite mountain peaks, pristine lakes and wild fauna including guanacos, condors and pumas, it is considered the 8 th Natural Wonder of the World. With 17 hours of daylight during summertime and purple-coloured skies during sunset it is a paradise for hiking and photography lovers!

Estancia Cerro Guido

The city of Puerto Natales, located just 1.5 hours south of the National Park, marks the connecting point to the famous Perito Moreno Glacier and the city of El Calafate in Argentina.

Easter Island

Land of mysteries

Rapa Nui: another world

Yes, Easter Island is part of Chile! It is one of the most isolated islands in the world located 3.700 km from Santiago in the middle of the Pacific Ocean. Land of mysteries and cradle of many legends, the island is famous for its sculptures, the “Moais”. They are iconic, unique and very curious stone figures that have captivated people from all over the world.

Moais

Scuba diving

Explore Rapa Nui, its abundant archaeology, volcanoes, grasslands and more than 1,000 Moais, silent witnesses of a complex ancient society.

Rapa Nui culture – copyright Sernatur

Meet indigenous people and learn about their traditions, a truly extraordinary experience. Apart from the archaeological visits, you may also go scuba diving, snorkelling or do some horseback riding in this remote and unparalleled destination.
